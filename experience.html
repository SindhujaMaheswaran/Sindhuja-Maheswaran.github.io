Coursework

Overview

This portfolio demonstrates proficiency across key data science program learning outcomes through practical, impactful projects. The projects included showcase the use of diverse technologies and methodologies to gather, analyze, and visualize data, generating insights that address societal, business, and environmental challenges. Skills applied include data visualization, predictive modeling, ethical consideration in model development, and effective communication with technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Data Collection, Storage, and Access

Across my projects, I effectively utilized Python, Tableau, and TensorFlow to manage and retrieve large datasets, supporting data-driven analyses and insights. For example, in the Crime Classification Based on Geographical Area project, I processed over a million records, efficiently handling data using Python and TensorFlow libraries to prepare and structure data for modeling. Similarly, in the Zillow House Price Prediction project,I used PySpark to manage and process extensive Zillow datasets, demonstrating my expertise in data storage and access mechanisms..

Insight Generation Across Contexts

Each project provided context-specific insights, showcasing my ability to cover the full data science lifecycle from data preparation to actionable outcomes. In the Crime Classification project, my work on classifying crime data based on location identified high-risk areas, offering valuable insights for public safety. Meanwhile, the Natural Disasters and Economic Impact in the USA project analyzed disaster trends, identifying economic impact patterns that can inform policy and community resilience strategies. The Zillow House Price Prediction project offered actionable insights on housing market trends, aiding stakeholders in strategic decision-making for property investments..

Visualization and Predictive Modeling

I applied advanced visualization and predictive modeling techniques to transform raw data into impactful insights. Tableau dashboards in the Natural Disasters and Economic Impact project enabled stakeholders to visually interpret complex data on disaster frequencies and severities. In Crime Classification, I developed predictive models, such as CNN, gradient boosting, and logistic regression, which improved classification accuracy by 70%, helping to identify high-risk zones. For the Zillow House Price Prediction project, I utilized models like ARIMA, KNN, and CNN for housing price forecasts, showcasing predictive modeling expertise in trend analysis and forecasting.

Programming Proficiency

My programming skills in Python, R, and PySpark enabled me to execute complex analyses, automate workflows, and build predictive models across projects. In Crime Classification, I relied on Python libraries (Sklearn, TensorFlow) to power model training and performance enhancement. The Zillow House Price Prediction project utilized PySpark for large-scale data handling and Python for implementing ARIMA and CNN models, highlighting my versatility with both statistical and deep learning frameworks..

Communication of Insights

Each project emphasized the importance of designing accessible visual dashboards and effectively presenting insights. For instance, in the Natural Disasters project, I designed visual dashboards with Tableau, making disaster-related data clear for non-technical stakeholders. Similarly, in Crime Classification, I communicated findings on high-risk areas through visualizations, making complex model results interpretable for public safety officials..

Ethics in Data Science

Throughout these projects, I addressed ethical considerations by prioritizing data transparency, privacy, and unbiased model outcomes. In Crime Classification, ethical considerations included addressing potential biases in crime data to ensure fair model predictions. Similarly, in the Natural Disasters and Economic Impact project, transparency and ethical data use were paramount, especially given the social and economic implications of disaster impact analysis. By implementing careful data handling practices, I ensured my work aligned with responsible data science standards.
