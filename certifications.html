Overview

This portfolio demonstrates proficiency across key data science program learning outcomes through practical, impactful projects. The projects included showcase the use of diverse technologies and methodologies to gather, analyze, and visualize data, generating insights that address societal, business, and environmental challenges. Skills applied include data visualization, predictive modeling, ethical consideration in model development, and effective communication with technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Data Collection, Storage, and Access

Across your projects, you effectively utilized Python, Tableau, and TensorFlow to manage and retrieve large datasets, supporting data-driven analyses and insights. Examples include the Crime Classification project, where over a million records were processed, and the Zillow House Price Prediction project, which leveraged PySpark to handle extensive datasets.

Insight Generation Across Contexts

Each project provided context-specific insights, from identifying high-risk areas in the Crime Classification project to analyzing economic impacts in the Natural Disasters project. These insights assist stakeholders in making strategic, data-driven decisions.

Visualization and Predictive Modeling

Advanced visualization and predictive modeling transformed raw data into insights. Projects like Natural Disasters and Economic Impact used Tableau for data visualization, while models such as CNN and ARIMA were utilized in predictive tasks for housing market trends.

Programming Proficiency

Proficiency in Python, R, and PySpark enabled complex analyses and model implementation. Python libraries like Sklearn and TensorFlow were applied in Crime Classification, while PySpark and ARIMA were used in the Zillow House Price Prediction project.

Communication of Insights

Insights were effectively communicated through dashboards and visualizations. For example, in the Natural Disasters project, dashboards in Tableau helped non-technical stakeholders interpret complex data, ensuring accessible, actionable information.

Ethics in Data Science

Ethical considerations were prioritized, including transparency, privacy, and bias mitigation. Crime Classification focused on fair model predictions, while the Natural Disasters project emphasized transparency given its societal impact.
